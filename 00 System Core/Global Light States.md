These are controlled by two things:
- **shadow belt alignment**
- **cloud layer opacity**

They determine **how much corelight reaches the island layer**. As well as the primary seasonal driver

---

### 1 — Total Shadow
[[Season Phases]] Stage: Cooling Phase
Shadow belt Density: solid
Cloud Density: N/A 

- global night
- strongest cooling period
- atmospheric convection weakens
- wind belts slow slightly
- sky becomes similar to nautical twilight


Notes
- corelight completely blocked by shadow belt
- this is the darkest condition the world experiences
- agriculture life relies on stored heat and moisture

---

### 2 — Double Filter
[[Season Phases]] Stage: Maximum Dim Phase
Shadow belt Density: moderate
Cloud Density: heavy 

- extremely low illumination
- photosynthesis slows
- temperature drops
- fog layers thicken
- navigation harder


Notes
- The gloomiest seasonal phase
- no shadows

---

### 3 — Diffuse Filter
[[Season Phases]] Stage: Low Energy Phase
Shadow belt Density: moderate
Cloud Density: light 

- bright twilight
- weaker heating
- cooler daytime temperatures
- longer dew/fog persistence


Notes
- constant hazy light
- weak shadows

---

### 4 — Cloud Filter
[[Season Phases]] Stage: Stable Equilibrium Phase
Shadow belt Density: light
Cloud Density: light 

- soft diffuse daylight
- stable moderate temperatures
- high humidity
- ideal agricultural conditions


Notes
- The most common normal state
- no shadows
---

### 4 — Heavy Cloud Filter
[[Season Phases]] Stage: Stable Equilibrium Phase
Shadow belt Density: light
Cloud Density: heavy 

- dim twilight
- weaker heating
- cooler daytime temperatures
- longer dew/fog persistence


Notes
- The most common normal state
- no shadows

---

### 5 — Clear 
[[Season Phases]] Stage: High Energy Phase
Shadow belt Density: none
Cloud Density: very light/none 

- brightest skies
- strongest convection
- stronger winds
- increased evaporation
- warmest period


Notes
- High energy climate phase
- risk of sunburn and sun damage
