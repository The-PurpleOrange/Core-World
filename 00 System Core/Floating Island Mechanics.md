## Overview

Floating islands remain suspended because their rock contains a **lift-mineral filament network** energized by radiation from the exposed planetary core.

When the mineral absorbs core radiation, energy spreads through the network and produces a **gravity-opposing force** across the island’s structure.

Islands stabilize at the altitude where total lift generated by the network balances the island’s total mass.

---

## Lift–Mass Equilibrium

Every floating body reaches a natural equilibrium point where:

lift produced by the mineral network = gravitational pull of the island

If lift temporarily exceeds gravity, the island rises.  
If lift drops below gravity, the island slowly descends.

Because radiation intensity decreases with distance from the core, islands naturally settle into a **stable floating band above the cloud sea**.

---

## Lift Network Structure

Lift mineral forms a microscopic filament lattice running through rock and soil.

This network:

- distributes energy across the entire island
- spreads lift forces through the structure
- reinforces the island mechanically

Because lift is distributed rather than concentrated in one point, large landmasses can remain stable without breaking apart.

---

## Crystal Energy Hubs

Within the filament network, dense crystal deposits act as **energy collectors**.

These crystals:

- absorb core radiation efficiently
- store large amounts of energy
- feed energy into the surrounding filament lattice

Islands containing more crystal deposits can maintain greater lift and may stabilize at higher altitudes.

---

## Altitude Layers

Different rock bodies settle at different heights depending on their **lift-mineral concentration relative to mass**.

High lift concentration  
→ floats higher

Moderate lift concentration  
→ floats within the main habitable band

Low lift concentration  
→ floats just above the cloud sea

These layers form the vertical structure of the floating world.

---

## Night and Shadow Effects

Lift crystals store energy absorbed during periods of radiation exposure.

When radiation decreases during night or shadow:

- stored energy continues to power the network
- lift output slowly declines
- islands may descend slightly

Because large islands contain vast mineral networks, their altitude changes only minimally during these cycles.

Smaller floating bodies experience larger fluctuations.

---

## Sub-Cloud Metal Bodies

Dense metal-rich meteors and ore bodies contain little or no lift mineral.

Without a filament network to distribute energy:

mass greatly exceeds available lift

These bodies cannot remain suspended and sink through the cloud layer, forming dense debris fields below the floating island layer.

These sub-cloud objects are major sources of metal ore.

---

## Fragmentation and New Islands

When pieces of rock break away from a floating island:

- fragments containing lift mineral may stabilize and become small floating bodies
- fragments without lift mineral sink toward the cloud sea

Over geological time this process contributes to the formation of drifting micro-islands.

---

## Summary

Floating islands exist because lift mineral forms an energy-powered network throughout rock that converts core radiation into a distributed gravity-opposing force.

Island altitude is determined by:

- lift-mineral concentration
- total island mass
- availability of core radiation

Together these factors maintain the stable layer of floating land above the global cloud sea.
