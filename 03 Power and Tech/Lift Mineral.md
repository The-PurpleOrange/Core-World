## Overview

Lift mineral is a crystalline material that converts **core radiation energy into a gravity-opposing force**.  
It occurs naturally throughout rock as a **fine filament network**, but can also form **large solid crystals**.

When exposed to core radiation the mineral absorbs energy, distributes it through the network, and generates lift that allows islands to remain suspended above the cloud sea.

---

## Forms

### Filament Network

Most lift mineral exists as a microscopic filament lattice running through rock and soil.

Properties

- spreads slowly through mineral material
- distributes absorbed energy through surrounding rock
- structurally reinforces floating islands
- provides baseline lift across large landmasses

The network acts as both a **structural binder** and an **energy distribution system**.

---

### Crystal Deposits

In some locations the mineral forms dense crystalline bodies.

Properties

- high energy absorption
- high lift potential
- large energy storage capacity

These crystals are the primary source used for **lift technology and vessel construction**.

---

## Lift Generation

Lift crystals generate a **gravity-opposing force** when energized.

The force is emitted **perpendicular to the crystal lattice planes**, meaning the direction of lift depends on the crystal’s internal alignment.

Because of this:

- well-aligned crystals produce strong, efficient lift
- irregular crystals produce weaker, scattered lift

---

## Natural Crystals

Wild crystals grow with irregular lattice orientations.

Consequences

- lift emission occurs in multiple directions
- some lift cancels internally
- overall lift efficiency is lower

These crystals still contribute to island buoyancy through the surrounding filament network.

---

## Crystal Cultivation

Crystals can be **grown and shaped deliberately**.

During growth:

- surfaces are shaved or cut
- lattice orientation is guided
- growth directions are controlled

This produces crystals where the lattice planes align in a consistent direction.

Benefits

- lift emission becomes coherent
- energy density increases
- smaller crystals can produce useful lift

These shaped crystals are used as the **lift cores of sky vessels**.

---

## Energy Absorption and Storage

Lift crystals absorb energy when exposed to core radiation.

Excess energy is stored within the crystal structure and slowly released when radiation decreases.

This allows floating bodies to remain airborne during:

- night cycles
- temporary shadow
- cloud cover

Large crystal masses store far more energy than small ones.

---

## Growth and Saturation

Crystal growth occurs when excess radiation energy is available.

Process

1. radiation absorbed by crystal
2. energy distributed through filament network
3. lift demand satisfied
4. excess energy promotes crystal expansion

As the network grows larger:

- energy must travel farther
- transfer losses increase
- additional growth becomes inefficient

Eventually the system stabilizes and crystal growth stops.

---

## Geological Role

Because the filament network spreads slowly through rock:

- islands maintain lift over geological time
- damaged regions can gradually regrow mineral structures
- fragments containing lift mineral may develop into new floating bodies

The mineral therefore behaves as a **self-maintaining buoyancy system embedded within planetary geology**.
