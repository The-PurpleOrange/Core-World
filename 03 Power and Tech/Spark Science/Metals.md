# Early–Mid Tech Metals and Alloys

## Copper

![https://i.etsystatic.com/19554270/r/il/95656c/3772540022/il_570xN.3772540022_20ht.jpg](https://i.etsystatic.com/19554270/r/il/95656c/3772540022/il_570xN.3772540022_20ht.jpg)

**Composition**
- Nearly pure copper.

**Pros**
- Easy to smelt and cast.
- Extremely workable (can cold hammer).
- Corrosion resistant.

**Cons**
- Very soft.
- Edge dulls quickly.
- Cannot hold strong weapon edges.

**Typical Uses**
- Early tools
- Decorative weapons
- Electrical conductors (if your world uses dynamos)
- Small mechanical parts
    

**Worldbuilding note**  
Often the **first metal civilization discovers**.

# Bronze Alloys

Bronze is where civilizations become truly metal-based.

## Tin Bronze

![https://microsites.museum.ie/bronzeagehandlingbox/images/header-object-spear.jpg](https://microsites.museum.ie/bronzeagehandlingbox/images/header-object-spear.jpg)

**Composition**
- Copper + 8–12% tin

**Pros**
- Much harder than copper.
- Casts extremely well.
- Good corrosion resistance.
- Holds an edge better than copper.

**Cons**
- Brittle if too much tin.
- Requires tin trade networks.

**Typical Uses**
- Swords
- Axes
- Spears
- Helmets
- Armor plates

**Real history**  
The entire **Bronze Age weapon system** used this alloy.

## Arsenical Bronze

![https://cdn11.bigcommerce.com/s-fme25/images/stencil/1280x1280/products/7117/107725/lur267hh__76910.1673495279.jpg?c=2](https://cdn11.bigcommerce.com/s-fme25/images/stencil/1280x1280/products/7117/107725/lur267hh__76910.1673495279.jpg?c=2)

![https://i.pinimg.com/736x/9f/55/38/9f553842c76b1fa4cfb44acf468d6019.jpg](https://i.pinimg.com/736x/9f/55/38/9f553842c76b1fa4cfb44acf468d6019.jpg)

**Composition**
- Copper + arsenic

**Pros**
- Harder than pure copper.
- Work hardens well.
- Naturally forms when smelting some ores.

**Cons**
- **Poisonous to produce**.
- Unstable composition.

**Typical Uses**
- Early bronze weapons
- Tools

**Worldbuilding note**  
Could lead to **sick smiths / cursed mines** lore.

**Composition**
- Copper + tin + phosphorus

**Pros**
- Stronger than normal bronze.
- Excellent fatigue resistance.
- Great for springs.

**Cons**
- Harder to produce historically.

**Typical Uses**
- Springs
- Bearings
- Mechanical gears

**Worldbuilding relevance**  
Very good for **mechanical technology cultures** like yours.

# Iron
## Wrought Iron

![https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/419HdngoRGL.jpg](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/419HdngoRGL.jpg)

**Composition**
- Iron with very low carbon.

**Pros**
- Tough and flexible.
- Resistant to cracking.
- Easy to forge.

**Cons**
- Soft edge.
- Cannot hold sharp blades well.

**Typical Uses**
- Tools
- Nails
- Chains
- Armor framework
- Structural metal

**Real history**  
Used widely before steel became common.

---
## Cast Iron

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Cast_iron_melting.JPG](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Cast_iron_melting.JPG)

**Composition**
- Iron with high carbon (~2–4%)

**Pros**
- Casts easily.
- Hard surface.

**Cons**
- Very brittle.
- Cannot be forged.

**Typical Uses**
- Cannon
- Heavy machine parts
- Counterweights

**Worldbuilding**  
Great for **industrial infrastructure**.

# Metal Tiers Progression

### Tier 1 — Early Metal Age

- Copper
- Arsenical bronze

Weapons: axes, daggers

---

### Tier 2 — Bronze Age Civilizations

- Tin bronze
- Brass

Weapons: swords, spears  
Armor: scale, helmets

---

### Tier 3 — Iron Age

- Wrought iron
- Early steel

Weapons: strong swords  
Armor: mail, lamellar

---

### Tier 4 — Mechanical Age

Fits your world’s **mechanical industry focus**.

- Phosphor bronze
- Steel    
- Brass
- Cast iron

Tools and machines.

---

# For Your Floating Island World

The most common metals would likely be:

**Common**

- bronze (tools)
- wrought iron (structures)
- brass (machines)

**High value**

- steel (weapons)
- phosphor bronze (springs)

**Heavy industry**

- cast iron (counterweights, machinery)
