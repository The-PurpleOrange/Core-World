# What Alloys Dominate Specific Industries

Historically each industry converges on specific metals because of physical properties.

---

# Weapons

Best materials:
- tin bronze
- steel
- meteoric iron (elite)

Why:
- good edge retention
- impact resistance
- ability to forge blades

Bronze weapons dominated for ~2000 years historically.

---

# Armor

Best materials:
- bronze
- wrought iron
- steel (elite)

Why:
- toughness
- ability to absorb impacts

Bronze armor was extremely common in ancient armies.

---

# Farming Tools

Best materials:
- wrought iron
- bronze

Why:
- durable
- easy to repair
- forgiving metal

Iron dominates here because **tools take abuse**.

---

# Gears and Machinery

Best materials:
- brass
- phosphor bronze

Why:
- low friction
- good wear resistance
- does not seize easily

This is why **clockwork and instruments use brass**.

---

# Bearings and Sliding Parts

Best materials:
- lead bronze
- bismuth bronze
- molybdochalkos

Why:
- self lubricating
- low friction
- absorbs vibration

These alloys are extremely common in mechanical engineering.

---

# Springs

Best materials:
- phosphor bronze
- steel

Why:
- fatigue resistance
- elasticity

Phosphor bronze is historically used in **clock springs**.

---

# Counterweights / Ballast

Best materials:
- lead
- cast iron

Why:
- density
- cheap casting

Perfect for gravity-storage systems or machinery weights.

---

# Precision Instruments

Best materials:
- brass
- nickel silver

Why:
- corrosion resistance
- stable machining
- smooth surfaces

This is why telescopes and scientific tools historically use brass.

---

# Ship Hardware / Moving Structures

Best materials:
- wrought iron
- brass
- bronze

Why:
- corrosion resistance
- structural strength

---


# Realistic Civilization Metal Distribution

## Common People (vast majority of population)

These are metals that **village smiths or traveling smiths can realistically produce**.

**Most common**
- copper
- simple tin bronze
- wrought iron

**Typical items**
- knives
- axes
- farming tools
- nails
- hinges
- cookware
- spearheads

**Armor**
- leather
- bronze helmets
- simple bronze scale

These metals dominate because they are **forgiving to produce and repair**.

---

## Skilled Regional Smiths

Found in **towns, trade hubs, and mining regions**.

They can control alloy mixtures and forge larger objects.

**Materials**
- higher quality bronze alloys
- brass
- cast iron
- arsenical bronze
- early steels

**Typical items**
- swords
- armor components
- complex tools
- locks
- pumps
- windmill mechanisms

---

## Specialist Metallurgists

Very rare craftsmen in major cities or industrial centers.

These people understand **precise alloying and complex furnace control**.

**Materials**
- phosphor bronze
- bismuth bronze
- lead bronze
- nickel silver
- patterned steel

**Typical items**
- precision machines
- scientific instruments
- high quality weapons
- mechanical systems

---

## Elite / Rare Materials

These materials exist but **are not manufactured by most smiths**.

They appear through trade, rare ores, or natural sources.

**Materials**
- meteoric iron
- tumbaga
- corinthian bronze
- precious metals

**Typical items**
- royal weapons
- ceremonial armor
- relics

Meteoric iron especially fits well in a sky world where metal may **literally fall from above**.

---
# Standard Mechanical Metal Stack

|Component|Typical Material|Why|
|---|---|---|
|Frame / structure|wrought iron|strong, tough, cheap|
|Shafts / axles|steel or iron|handles torque and bending|
|Gears|brass or bronze|low friction against steel|
|Bearings / bushings|bronze alloys|prevents shaft seizure|
|Heavy sliding surfaces|molybdochalkos or lead bronze|self-lubricating|
|Springs|steel or phosphor bronze|high fatigue resistance|
|Fasteners|iron or steel|strong and easy to forge|
|Instruments|brass or nickel silver|corrosion resistant, precise|
