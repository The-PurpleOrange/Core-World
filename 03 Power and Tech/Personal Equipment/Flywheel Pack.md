# Flywheel Pack

## Overview

The Flywheel Pack is a backpack-mounted portable mechanical energy storage system used to power heavy handheld tools and equipment.  
It stores energy in the rotational motion of a spinning flywheel and releases that energy through a controlled gear system to drive tools directly or generate electricity.

Flywheel Packs are the primary portable power source for demanding work where spring packs are insufficient.

Common applications include:

- handheld mining drills
- cutting tools
- portable winches
- temporary work lights
- small field machinery

The system is worn on the back to distribute weight and protect the flywheel inside a reinforced casing.

| Pack type               | Rotor mass | RPM | Energy  |

---

## Energy Storage Principle

Energy is stored as rotational kinetic energy in a spinning mass.

Where:
- E = stored energy
- I = rotational inertia of the flywheel
- ω = angular velocity

Increasing the flywheel mass, radius, or spin speed increases stored energy.

---

## Typical System Layout

A Flywheel Pack contains:

- flywheel rotor
- reinforced containment drum in event of failure
- main bearings
- clutch assembly
- output shaft
- gear reduction system
- centrifugal governor
- external power coupling
- braking lever

---

## Charging Methods

Flywheel Packs can be charged through several mechanical sources:

- hand crank
- fold-out pedal drive while standing
- stationary winding station
- connection to torque lines
- wind-powered charging rigs

Charging typically takes 1–3 minutes of strong cranking or a shorter time when connected to larger mechanical systems.

---

## Operational Characteristics

Advantages:

- high energy density compared to springs
- smooth torque delivery
- rapid recharge
- durable mechanical design

Limitations:

- heavy
- dangerous if casing fails
- power output drops as flywheel slows
- requires governors and clutches to control speed

---

## Cultural and Industrial Role

Flywheel Packs are a defining tool of heavy labour industries.

They are widely used by:

- underside miners
- industrial mechanics
- salvage crews
- military engineers

In many regions they are considered essential field equipment, enabling powerful mechanical tools to operate far from stationary power systems.

They represent the peak of portable mechanical power technology in a torque-dominant industrial world.

# Raw Math

**All math is based on the material assumed to be standard steel**

Energy formula (for a ring pair):  
E = ½ · m_total · r² · ω²
- **ring-style rotors**
- **radius = 0.15 m**
- **mass listed = total for both wheels**
