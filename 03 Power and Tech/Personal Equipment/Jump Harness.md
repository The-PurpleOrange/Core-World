## Overview

The jump harness is a personal mobility system designed to convert stored mechanical energy into a powerful assisted jump.

The harness itself does not store energy.  
Instead, it draws power from an attached energy pack such as:

- [[Spring Drive Pack]]
- [[Flywheel Pack]]

This modular system allows the same harness to be used with different power sources, as well as different strengths

Jump harnesses are commonly used by couriers, scouts, and military personnel operating in highly vertical terrain.

---

## Function

The harness converts rotational or spring energy from the backpack unit into a rapid mechanical impulse via a telescopic pole that boosts the user's jump.

The jump harness only provides a brief impulse during takeoff and does not allow sustained propulsion.

Force Path: springs → push strut → ground → pack frame → harness → hips → body

---

## System Layout

A typical jump harness contains:

- Pack frame mounted to hip belt and leg loops
  
-  **Deployable push-struts**
	- telescoping pole under the pack
    - ~30–40 cm stroke
    - rubber or metal foot pad**

- **Trigger + governor**
    - prevents instant “kick”
    - releases energy progressively

---

## Operational Characteristics

Advantages:

- kJ delivered can be adjusted before equipping
- rapid traversal of broken terrain
- ability to clear large gaps
- useful in dense vertical environments
- works on very small platforms
- no need for plant holes or sockets

Limitations:

- single impulse only
- requires skill to control landing
- repeated use causes fatigue

### Operating Sequence 

1. user takes a running start
2. trigger activates
3. push-strut plants on the ground
4. springs release through the strut
5. pack pushes upward through the harness
6. strut retracts and system must be rewound

## Jump Pack Performance

The following is based around a vertical distance of 2m
This is equivalent to a vertical launch speed of **~6.26 m/s**

Horizontal distance while running:
- **4 m/s**: **~7.6m** (up from 2.4m)
- **5 m/s**: **~8.5m** (up from 3.0m)
- **6 m/s**: **~9.4m** (up from 3.6m)
