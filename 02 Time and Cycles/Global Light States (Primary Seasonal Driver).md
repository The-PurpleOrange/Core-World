These are controlled by two things:
- **shadow belt alignment**
- **cloud layer opacity**

They determine **how much corelight reaches the island layer**. As well as the primary seasonal driver

---

### 1 — Total Shadow

Corelight completely blocked by the shadow belt.
- global night
- strongest cooling period
- atmospheric convection weakens
- wind belts slow slightly
- bioluminescence / moons dominate sky

Notes
- this is the darkest condition the world experiences
- agriculture life relies on stored heat and moisture

---

### 2 — Diffuse Shadow Day

Low-density shadow belt covering the core during the usual daylight period.
- dim twilight
- weaker heating
- cooler daytime temperatures
- longer dew/fog persistence

Notes
- constant hazy light
- weak shadows

---

### 3 — Double Filter

Low-density shadow belt **plus heavy cloud filtering**.
- extremely low illumination
- photosynthesis slows
- temperature drops
- fog layers thicken
- navigation harder

Notes
- The gloomiest seasonal phase
- no shadows

---

### 4 — Cloud Filter Only

Shadow belt not covering the core but light filtered through cloud layer.
- soft diffuse daylight
- stable moderate temperatures
- high humidity
- ideal agricultural conditions

Notes
- The most common normal state
- no shadows

---

### 5 — Clear Corelight

Corelight reaches islands without major obstruction.
- brightest skies
- strongest convection
- stronger winds
- increased evaporation
- warmest period

Notes
- High energy climate phase
- risk of sunburn and sun damage
