## Overview  
  
Agriculture on the floating islands depends primarily on three factors:  
  
- temperature (set by global climate bands)  
- moisture availability (set by atmospheric circulation)  
- island altitude relative to the cloud sea  
  
The most productive farming regions occur where **moderate temperatures combine with reliable moisture**.  
  
Because islands drift through atmospheric belts, agricultural regions may experience gradual long-term climate shifts, but the most stable farming zones occur on **anchored or slow-drifting islands**.  
  
---  
  
## Primary Agricultural Regions  
  
### Temperate Belt Farmlands  
  
Location    
Central portion of the temperate habitable belt.  
  
Conditions  
  
- moderate temperatures  
- stable growing seasons  
- balanced rainfall  
- manageable winds  
  
Typical Crops  
  
- wheat  
- barley  
- legumes  
- root crops  
- orchard fruits  
- vineyards  
  
This region supports the majority of large-scale farming and permanent settlements.  
  
---  
  
### Cloud-Edge Agriculture  
  
Location    
Islands positioned just above the cloud sea.  
  
Conditions  
  
- frequent fog  
- high humidity  
- regular condensation  
- mild temperatures  
  
Water often reaches plants through **fog drip and surface condensation**, allowing crops to grow even when rainfall is limited.  
  
Typical Crops  
  
- leafy vegetables  
- tubers  
- grains  
- fruit trees  
- moisture-tolerant crops  
  
These regions can be extremely productive due to the high fog moisture but may experience unstable weather.  
  
---  
  
### Seasonal Rain Belt Farming  
  
Location    
Regions intersected by major storm tracks.  
  
Conditions  
  
- alternating dry and wet periods  
- occasional heavy rainfall  
- strong winds during storm passages  
  
Typical Crops  
  
- hardy grains  
- legumes  
- grazing pastures  
  
These areas often support mixed agriculture with both farming and livestock.  
  
---  
  
## Marginal Agricultural Zones  
  
Some regions support limited agriculture but present challenges.  
  
### Dry Steppe Regions  
  
Characteristics  
  
- irregular rainfall  
- strong winds  
- periodic drought  
  
Typical Use  
  
- grazing animals  
- drought-resistant grains  
- seasonal farming  
  
---  
  
### Highland Agriculture  
  
Location    
High altitude islands above the main temperate belt.  
  
Conditions  
  
- cooler temperatures  
- stronger winds  
- thinner air  
- shorter growing seasons  
  
Typical Crops  
  
- hardy grains  
- cold-resistant vegetables  
- grazing livestock  
  
---  
  
## Poor Agricultural Regions  
  
Certain environments are generally unsuitable for farming.  
  
### Radiant Pole Regions  
  
Challenges  
  
- excessive heat  
- unstable storms  
- poor soil stability  
  
Human activity here focuses more on mining, exploration, or trade routes.  
  
---  
  
### Polar Shadow Regions  
  
Challenges  
  
- minimal light  
- extremely cold temperatures  
- slow biological growth  
  
Agriculture is extremely limited or impossible.  
  
---  
  
## Agricultural Stability  
  
The most stable farming systems develop where three conditions align:  
  
- moderate core exposure  
- regular but not excessive rainfall  
- predictable atmospheric circulation  
  
These regions typically occur in the **middle temperate belt**, making it the agricultural heart of the world.  
  
---  
  
## Summary  
  
The most productive farming climates include:  
  
- temperate belt farmlands  
- cloud-edge agriculture zones  
- seasonal rain belt farming regions  
  
Marginal areas support grazing or specialized crops, while extreme climates near the poles are largely unsuitable for agriculture.
