# Early–Mid Tech Metals and Alloys

## Copper

![https://i.etsystatic.com/19554270/r/il/95656c/3772540022/il_570xN.3772540022_20ht.jpg](https://i.etsystatic.com/19554270/r/il/95656c/3772540022/il_570xN.3772540022_20ht.jpg)

**Composition**
- Nearly pure copper.

**Pros**
- Easy to smelt and cast.
- Extremely workable (can cold hammer).
- Corrosion resistant.

**Cons**
- Very soft.
- Edge dulls quickly.
- Cannot hold strong weapon edges.

**Typical Uses**
- Early tools
- Decorative weapons
- Electrical conductors (if your world uses dynamos)
- Small mechanical parts
    

**Worldbuilding note**  
Often the **first metal civilization discovers**.

# Bronze Alloys

Bronze is where civilizations become truly metal-based.

## Tin Bronze

![https://microsites.museum.ie/bronzeagehandlingbox/images/header-object-spear.jpg](https://microsites.museum.ie/bronzeagehandlingbox/images/header-object-spear.jpg)

**Composition**
- Copper + 8–12% tin

**Pros**
- Much harder than copper.
- Casts extremely well.
- Good corrosion resistance.
- Holds an edge better than copper.

**Cons**
- Brittle if too much tin.
- Requires tin trade networks.

**Typical Uses**
- Swords
- Axes
- Spears
- Helmets
- Armor plates

**Real history**  
The entire **Bronze Age weapon system** used this alloy.

![https://cdn11.bigcommerce.com/s-fme25/images/stencil/1280x1280/products/7117/107725/lur267hh__76910.1673495279.jpg?c=2](https://cdn11.bigcommerce.com/s-fme25/images/stencil/1280x1280/products/7117/107725/lur267hh__76910.1673495279.jpg?c=2)

**Composition**
- Copper + arsenic

**Pros**
- Harder than pure copper.
- Work hardens well.

**Cons**
- **Poisonous to produce**.
- Unstable composition.

**Typical Uses**
- Early bronze weapons
- Tools

**Worldbuilding note**  
Could lead to **sick smiths / cursed mines** lore.

# Iron
## Wrought Iron

![https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/419HdngoRGL.jpg](https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/419HdngoRGL.jpg)

**Composition**
- Iron with very low carbon.

**Pros**
- Tough and flexible.
- Resistant to cracking.
- Easy to forge.

**Cons**
- Soft edge.
- Cannot hold sharp blades well.

**Typical Uses**
- Tools
- Nails
- Chains
- Armor framework
- Structural metal

**Real history**  
Used widely before steel became common.

---
## Cast Iron

![https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Cast_iron_melting.JPG](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c6/Cast_iron_melting.JPG)

**Composition**
- Iron with high carbon (~2–4%)

**Pros**
- Casts easily.
- Hard surface.

**Cons**
- Very brittle.
- Cannot be forged.

**Typical Uses**
- Cannon
- Heavy machine parts
- Counterweights

**Worldbuilding**  
Great for **industrial infrastructure**.

## Meteoric Iron (Nickel-Iron)

![https://www.militaria.co.za/nmb/uploads/monthly_08_2019/post-3496-0-66993800-1566732778.jpg](https://www.militaria.co.za/nmb/uploads/monthly_08_2019/post-3496-0-66993800-1566732778.jpg)

**Composition**
- Iron + 5–20% nickel.

**Pros**
- Naturally steel-like.
- Extremely tough.
- Can be forged.
- Distinct **Widmanstätten crystal pattern**

**Cons**
- Rare.
- Hard to shape.

**Uses**
- Elite weapons
- Ritual blades

**Real history**  
Used before iron smelting (e.g., Tutankhamun’s dagger).

**Worldbuilding**  
Great prestige metal for **sky miners**.

- Sling bullets
- Plumbing

# Molybdochalkos

**Composition**

- Copper + lead (sometimes with small tin).

**Historical reference**  
Mentioned in Greek texts as _molybdochalkos_ (“lead copper”).

**Properties**

Pros
- Very easy to cast.
- Self-lubricating because of lead content.
- Good vibration damping.

Cons
- Weak structurally.
- Cannot hold edges.
- Lead toxicity.

**Typical Uses**
- Bearings
- Bushings
- Sliding surfaces
- Decorative cast objects

**Worldbuilding use**

Excellent for **machinery parts** where friction reduction matters.  
Fits extremely well in a mechanical civilization.

## Bismuth Bronze

![https://images.prismic.io/xometry-marketing/e5b77fbd-d2c9-4b5c-b487-988b3fb44fa5_bismuth%2Bbronze%2Bbushings.jpg?auto=compress%2Cformat&fit=max&h=486&rect=166%2C0%2C667%2C667&w=486](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-1/oNGItlqvVP0VmGE7GoghOd0-iLRtjYGoGn64MQlLFoQu5tCTUcL2hINhEpwiNM3kLXEkcL8KLqMFXKbRVVyH_5xWTsiPEXG0c5l8IVYWgN1y1BXEvzWbJwFC3MebNy8asKdL4caXbHfr5kLvCJI-SQ)

**Composition**
- Copper
- Tin (like normal bronze)
- ~1–6% **bismuth**

**Pros**
- Excellent **castability** (fills molds very well)
- **Low friction** metal (good bearing material)
- Non-toxic compared to lead
- Good corrosion resistance

**Cons**
- Slightly **more brittle** than standard bronze
- Not ideal for weapons or impact tools
- Can crack if forged heavily

**Typical Uses**
- Bearings
- Bushings
- Valve components
- Decorative cast parts
- Low-friction mechanical pieces

**Worldbuilding**

Very believable in a **mechanical civilization** where lead substitutes might be desirable for machinery.
