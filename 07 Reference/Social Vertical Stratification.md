Civilization in the floating world is structured primarily by **altitude**, not geography.

Floating islands stabilize at different heights depending on the balance between lift mineral and total mass. 0

Each altitude band experiences different combinations of:

- wind exposure
- rainfall
- fog
- storm intensity
- available resources

As a result, societies tend to cluster within specific vertical zones where environmental conditions are most stable.

Territory disputes therefore revolve less around land area and more around **control of strategic altitude bands**.

---

# Major Vertical Bands

## High Sky

Characteristics

- cold temperatures
- thin moisture levels
- powerful high-altitude winds
- sparse ecosystems

Settlements

- navigation towers
- astronomical observatories
- long-range signal stations
- weather monitoring platforms

Society

- very small populations
- highly specialized professions
- isolated research or navigation communities

High sky settlements are rare but valuable due to their **clear visibility and long-distance observation capability**.

---

## Mid Sky (Primary Civilization Zone)

Characteristics

- balanced winds
- moderate rainfall
- relatively stable climate
- limited fog compared to lower altitudes

Settlements

- major cities
- drifting trade hubs
- large agricultural islands
- long-term settlements

Society

- largest population centers
- major political powers
- stable economic systems
- long-distance trade networks

Most permanent civilizations develop in the mid-sky region because it provides the best balance between wind exposure and moisture.

Islands in this band receive enough rainfall for agriculture while avoiding the constant storm exposure near the cloud sea.

Many **belt islands** migrate through this region as they follow global wind circulation lanes. 1

These drifting island chains often function as moving trade corridors.

---

## Cloud Edge

Characteristics

- heavy fog and mist
- strong convection
- frequent rainfall
- rising storm towers from the cloud sea

Settlements

- farming islands
- moisture harvesting settlements
- storm energy infrastructure
- descent ports for mining operations

Society

- smaller populations
- industry focused on farming, water capture, and energy
- high maintenance settlements

Islands near the cloud layer receive constant moisture from fog lift and cloud updraft spray. 2

Vegetation grows rapidly in this region, making it ideal for agriculture.

However these islands are also exposed to powerful storm systems rising from the cloud sea.

Because of the abundance of water and fertile soils, cloud-edge islands often function as **major water and food export centers** supplying larger mid-sky cities.

These islands are economically important but less comfortable for long-term habitation.

---

## Upper Cloud Towers

Characteristics

- massive storm columns rising from the cloud sea
- violent turbulence
- strong updrafts
- intense lightning activity

Settlements

- none permanent

Use

- experimental energy harvesting
- hazardous flight corridors
- atmospheric research

Storm towers occasionally rise high enough to intersect the island layer and are among the most dangerous regions of the atmosphere.

---

## Cloud Sea

Characteristics

- dense global cloud ocean
- violent internal storms
- near zero visibility
- powerful vertical convection

The cloud sea functions as the planet’s primary water reservoir and atmospheric engine. 3

It forms a massive atmospheric layer roughly **6 km deep** separating the floating islands from the hotter lower atmosphere. 4

Travel within this region is extremely dangerous.

---

## Sub-Cloud Region

Characteristics

- strong heat columns from the planetary core
- intense updraft currents
- drifting debris fields
- concentrations of metal-rich meteors

Use

- extreme mining operations
- resource recovery expeditions

Metallic bodies lacking lift mineral cannot remain suspended and accumulate below the cloud layer. 5

Some industrial nations conduct dangerous **cloud-dive mining operations** to recover these materials.

Recovered resources may include:

- meteoric iron
- nickel-rich metals
- rare dense alloys

# Three-Layer Civilization Model

Most societies in the floating world organize around a three-layer economic structure.

## High Sky — Knowledge Layer

Functions

- navigation infrastructure
- astronomical observation
- weather prediction
- long-distance communication

Population

- very small
- specialized personnel

---

## Mid Sky — Civilization Layer

Functions

- major cities
- trade hubs
- permanent settlements
- political centers

Population

- majority of the global population

---

## Cloud Edge — Resource Layer

Functions

- agriculture
- water harvesting
- storm energy
- mining descent points
- food and water export

Population

- smaller industrial communities

---

# Strategic Importance of Altitude

Control of altitude bands determines access to critical resources.

Islands positioned just above the cloud sea provide access to:

- reliable water supply
- fertile agricultural land
- storm energy systems
- mining access to sub-cloud metals

These islands function as the equivalent of:

- fertile river deltas
- major ports
- industrial resource zones

Because islands drift over time, strategic geography constantly shifts.

Civilizations must track atmospheric currents and island migration patterns in order to maintain control over key altitude zones.
