
## 1) Backpack Flywheel (Heavy Tools)

Purpose

- Power drills
- Run small machinery
- Provide steady torque output

Storage Principle  
Rotational kinetic energy.

E=12Iω2E = \tfrac12 I \omega^2E=21​Iω2

Where:

- I = rotational inertia (mass + radius)
- ω = angular velocity

Characteristics

- High energy for size (compared to springs)
- Smooth output
- Recharge by crank, pedal, or torque line
- Good for 2–10 minutes heavy load

Best Use

- Backpack-mounted industrial tools
- Shared crew equipment

Weakness

- Heavy
- Dangerous if casing fails
- Requires governor

## 2) Spring Storage (Handheld Devices)

Purpose

- Torches
- Small instruments
- Igniters
- Signal lamps

Storage Principle  
Elastic deformation.

E=12kx2E = \tfrac12 kx^2E=21​kx2

Characteristics

- Compact
- Safe failure mode
- Lower energy density than flywheel
- Easy to swap (spring drum “magazines”)

Best Use

- Personal gear
- Short duration devices
- Lightweight applications

Weakness

- Output torque drops as it unwinds
- Needs gearing for stable output

# Converting Mechanical to Electrical

Electricity is secondary, so conversion is simple and local.

## Method 1 – Dynamo (Rotating Coil + Magnet)

Most common and reliable.

- Mechanical shaft spins rotor
- Magnetite-rich sky-iron used for magnets
- Copper or alloy windings
- Produces current proportional to spin speed

Voltage roughly scales with speed:

V∝ωV \propto \omegaV∝ω

Used For

- Torches
- Heating elements
- Indicator lamps
- Instrument lighting

---

## Method 2 – Small Permanent Magnet Generator

Miniature version of above.

- Built into torch body
- Spring unwinds → spins micro-generator
- Gear train increases RPM

Very plausible for handheld devices.

---

## Method 3 – Piezoelectric Inserts (Optional Native Material)

If you keep your special ore:

- Pressure generates small spark
- Good for ignition
- Not good for sustained lighting

Used For

- Detonator ignition
- Signal clicks
- Emergency spark
