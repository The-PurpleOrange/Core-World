
Electricity is secondary, so conversion is simple and local.

## Method 1 – Dynamo (Rotating Coil + Magnet)

Most common and reliable.

- Mechanical shaft spins rotor
- Magnetite-rich sky-iron used for magnets
- Copper or alloy windings
- Produces current proportional to spin speed

Voltage roughly scales with speed:

V∝ωV \propto \omegaV∝ω

Used For

- Torches
- Heating elements
- Indicator lamps
- Instrument lighting

---

## Method 2 – Small Permanent Magnet Generator

Miniature version of above.

- Built into torch body
- Spring unwinds → spins micro-generator
- Gear train increases RPM

Very plausible for handheld devices.

---

## Method 3 – Piezoelectric Inserts (Optional Native Material)

If you keep your special ore:

- Pressure generates small spark
- Good for ignition
- Not good for sustained lighting

Used For

- Detonator ignition
- Signal clicks
- Emergency spark
