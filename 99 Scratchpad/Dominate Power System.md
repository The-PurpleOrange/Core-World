CORE RULES

- tech level: pre-combustion
- energy sources: wind, gravity, pressure, heat, motion
- power must visibly connect to environment
- location determines available technology

GLOBAL FORCES (ALL MACHINES TRACE TO ONE)

1. radiant core pressure → heat + updraft force
2. atmospheric current belts → horizontal wind force
3. lunar tidal pull → slow rocking force
4. pressure gradient → airflow between layers
5. island drift → relative motion

---

PRIMARY POWER SYSTEMS

1. THERMAL UPDRAFT TOWERS  
    source: core heat columns  
    function: rising air spins turbine  
    best for: industry, heavy machinery  
    locations: anchored islands

traits

- constant power
- scalable
- stationary infrastructure

---

2. BELT WIND DRIVES  
    source: constant relative wind from moving islands  
    function: rotors or sail drums spin from airflow  
    best for: trade hubs, mobile settlements  
    locations: belt islands

traits

- continuous energy
- portable industry
- easy to repair

---

3. CLOUD GRAVITY ENGINES  
    source: sudden rain columns from cloudfalls  
    function: falling water drives wheel/piston  
    best for: burst production + storage charging  
    locations: storm corridors

traits

- intermittent
- extremely strong during storms
- used seasonally

---

4. TETHER DIFFERENTIAL ENGINES  
    source: different drift speeds between islands  
    function: tether tension spins drum  
    best for: villages + local industry  
    locations: clusters + near-alignment regions

traits

- simple engineering
- low infrastructure
- highly iconic tech

---

5. PRESSURE WELL SYSTEMS  
    source: air pressure difference between altitudes  
    function: airflow through pipes drives turbines  
    best for: automation + distributed mechanical grids  
    locations: islands with access shafts below cloud layer

traits

- steady
- invisible power source
- good for pumps + small machines

---

6. GRAVITY STORAGE NETWORKS  
    source: lifted weights storing energy  
    function: stored potential energy released later  
    best for: stabilizing intermittent power  
    locations: everywhere

traits

- universal standard
- replaces electrical grid concept
- allows slow engines to run fast machines

TECHNOLOGICAL GEOGRAPHY

anchored islands → thermal industry centers  
belt islands → wind-powered trade cities  
clusters → tether-driven villages  
storm belts → seasonal industry zones  
high sky → pressure tech settlements

technology varies by region instead of era

---

CIVILIZATION CONSEQUENCES

- cities migrate to energy sources
- engineers are elite professionals
- maps show current lanes, not landmasses
- wars fought over airflow corridors
- trade based on timing alignment, not distance
