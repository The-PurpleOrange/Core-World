## Environmental Baseline

- Ambient temperature ~37°C
- Constant updraft and wind shear
- Dust-heavy atmosphere
- Occasional steam bursts
- Free-breath exposure limit ~20 minutes (light work)
- Heavy work requires supported air

## Phase 1 – Scout Descent

- Scout descends light via rope
- Wears heat layers + filter mask
- Carries small air tank for short recovery breaks
- Identifies ore seam and stable anchor points
- Tests rock integrity
- Installs underside anchors
- Rigs traverse/belly line
- Returns to surface

Threats

- Heat exhaustion
- Swinging in wind shear
- Brittle rock failure
- Dust inhalation

Solutions

- Travel light
- Short exposure window
- Air tank used only for recovery pauses
- Rapid ascent if symptoms appear

## Phase 2 – Rig Deployment

- Main platform lowered on hoist
- Belly line pulls platform under island
- Tag line prevents rotation
- Air line routed through cloud band if used
- Platform stabilized below seam

Threats

- Pendulum swing
- Anchor stress
- Line twist

Solutions

- Three-line control (hoist + belly + tag)
- Slow descent
- Redundant anchors

## Phase 3 – Work Setup

- Heavy rope net deployed beneath seam
- Fine mesh or canvas bucket liner installed
- Tools staged
- Air system activated for heavy drilling

Threats

- Heat load
- Dust
- Static buildup
- Surface delamination

Solutions

- Supplied air for sustained work
- Hydration cycles
- Grounded tools
- Work in narrow vertical sections

## Phase 4 – Extraction

Stable ore

- Remove host rock
- Controlled prying or small charges
- Chunks fall into net

Brittle ore

- Cut relief slots
- Remove smaller segments
- Mesh liner catches fragments

Threats

- Lateral slab shear
- Fragment spray
- Net overload

Solutions

- Avoid wide undercuts
- Incremental removal
- Multi-point net suspension
- Surface crew monitors load tension

## Phase 5 – Recovery

- Net detached once full
- Surface crew reels net upward
- Platform remains in position
- Cycle repeats

Threats

- Overweight lift
- Wind shift
- Debris cascade

Solutions

- Load estimation
- Clear signal system
- Pause during heavy shear gusts

Operational tone

- Harsh but survivable
- Physically demanding
- Heat constrained
- Mechanically controlled
- Yield dependent on care
