## Overview  
  
The planet’s Climate zones are primarily organised into **broad latitudinal climate bands**.
This is due primarily to the radiant poles constant core exposure (being the main driving engine for the [[01 - Planetary Climate Structure]]).
  
Because the bright pole receives constant core radiation and the dark pole receives none, temperature gradually decreases from north to south. This produces large-scale **latitudinal climate zones** across the world.
  
Within these bands, **atmospheric circulation and island geography modify rainfall and local conditions**, creating regional variations without changing the overall global structure.  
  
---  
  
## Global Biome Bands  
  
### Radiant Pole Drylands  
  
Location    
Near the center of the permanent bright pole.  
  
Characteristics  
  
- extremely strong core radiation    
- very high temperatures    
- intense vertical air movement    
- limited surface moisture    
  
Environment  
  
- exposed rock and mineral terrain    
- sparse vegetation adapted to heat    
- occasional extreme storm activity farther from the pole center    
  
This region is harsh and unstable but can still support specialized ecosystems.  
  
---  
  
### Northern Storm Belt  
  
Location    
South of the radiant pole within the upper portion of the hot zone.  
  
Characteristics  
  
- strong convection    
- frequent storms    
- heavy rainfall    
- warm temperatures    
  
Environment  
  
- dense forests    
- rapid plant growth    
- high biodiversity    
  
This belt acts as one of the planet’s main **sources of rainfall and atmospheric moisture**.  
  
---  
  
### Temperate Habitable Belt  
  
Location    
Across the middle half of the planet.  
  
Characteristics  
  
- moderate temperatures    
- stable climates    
- variable rainfall depending on atmospheric circulation    
  
Environment  
  
- grasslands    
- forests    
- fertile soils    
- most agricultural regions    
  
This band contains the majority of the planet’s civilizations due to its balanced climate and high capacity for agriculture.  
  
---  
  
### Southern Cool Belt  
  
Location    
Northern edge of the cold zone approaching the dark pole.  
  
Characteristics  
  
- cooler temperatures    
- reduced sunlight exposure    
- moderate but declining precipitation    
  
Environment  
  
- cold grasslands    
- hardy forests    
- seasonal snow in colder regions    
  
Agriculture becomes more difficult toward the southern edge of this band.  
  
---  
  
### Polar Shadow Region  
  
Location    
The permanent dark pole.  
  
Characteristics  
  
- minimal direct core light    
- extremely cold temperatures    
- very stable atmospheric conditions    
- low moisture levels    
  
Environment  
  
- ice fields    
- frozen rock    
- sparse biological activity    
  
This is the coldest and least hospitable region of the planet.

## Regional Climate Variation  
  
Although global climates follow horizontal bands, several factors create **regional biome diversity** within each zone.  
  
Important modifiers include:  
  
- atmospheric wind belts    
- storm corridors    
- island altitude    
- rain shadows created by large islands    
- proximity to the cloud sea moisture source    
  
These influences can create wetter or drier areas within the same global climate band.  
  
---  
  
## Biome Distribution Principles  
  
The planet’s ecosystems follow a layered structure:  
  
1. **Core exposure determines the global temperature bands.**    
2. **Atmospheric circulation controls moisture distribution.**    
3. **Local island geography shapes regional ecosystems.**  
  
This structure allows the world to maintain clear large-scale climate zones while still supporting diverse environments across the floating islands.

## Summary  
  
Major global biome bands include:  
  
- radiant pole drylands    
- northern storm belt rain regions    
- temperate habitable belt ecosystems    
- southern cool belt environments    
- polar shadow regions    
  
These large-scale zones form the basic ecological structure of the planet, with local variation occurring inside each band.
