  
A long-period atmospheric event where the cloud sea becomes significantly darker and thicker for a short period.  
  
Frequency  
Occurs every few years. Often following a period of 20-60 days of significantly less global cloud cover.
  
Duration  
Typically several months.  
  
Possible causes to explore  
- MINE The upper and lower cloud systems shift for a brief period of days, leading to extremely dense upper atmosphere clouds and less dense/lack of cloud cover in the cloud sea
- large scale convection imbalance  
- increased particulate injection into cloud layer  
- tidal effects from moons  
- chemical or biological bloom within cloud sea  
- periodic vertical mixing of lower cloud layers  
  
Observed effects  
- period of intense light with maybe increased rainfall, maybe the dryer south region gets heavily increased rain for the some 20-40 day period?
- global light reduction  
- temperature drop of approximately 2–4 °C  
- slower convection  
- increased fog persistence  
- reduced visibility across the sky  
  
Cultural consequences  
- agricultural stress periods  
- navigation hazards  
- superstition or ritual calendars  
- possible famine cycles if prolonged  
  
Note  
This event is separate from normal seasonal cycles and overlays the regular climate system.
