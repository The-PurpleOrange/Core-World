Potential long-term planetary cycles affecting the lift-mineral network and island geology.  
  
These are not climate seasons but slow environmental cycles that may occur over decades or centuries.  
  
Possible drivers  
- lift-mineral saturation cycles  
- slow changes in radiation absorption efficiency  
- crust stress redistribution in floating island lattice  
- large crystal growth phases  
- periodic fragmentation of overstressed island masses  
  
Possible observable effects  
- increased rockfalls from island undersides  
- formation of new micro-islands  
- sudden crystal growth veins  
- rare large island fractures  
  
This concept could explain occasional geological upheavals in an otherwise stable floating world.
